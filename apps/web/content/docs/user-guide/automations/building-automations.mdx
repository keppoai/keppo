## Start with a concrete operator outcome

The best automation prompts describe:

- the trigger or starting signal
- the systems involved
- what Keppo should produce
- what still needs human approval

Bad: "Handle support."

Better: "Read unread Gmail threads in the support inbox, draft a response using our policy, and require approval before sending."

## Builder stages

Keppo’s staged builder is meant to preserve context instead of forcing you through hidden setup detours.

- Draft generation captures the workflow idea.
- Setup questions resolve missing provider details or policy choices.
- Review keeps the resulting write surface explicit.

## Approval-first mindset

If an action changes a real system, assume approval is needed until the team has enough confidence to relax it with rules. Keppo is designed to help you earn automation trust, not bypass it.

<Callout title="Review the diff, not just the goal" tone="tip">
Two automations can sound similar in natural language but differ dramatically in write surface. Always inspect the concrete actions, prompts, and destinations before activation.
</Callout>

## Signs the draft is ready

- The trigger is specific.
- The write actions are understandable.
- The approval reviewer can tell what will happen next.
- The run detail page would make sense to a teammate who did not author the automation.
